Intro

I was born on December 30, 1995 in Paris, the city of lights. At 19, driven by a deep need to give meaning to my life, I made the bold choice to leave my family to join the army. It was an instinctive decision, almost obvious, as the feeling of uselessness weighed on me. For three years, I served as a fighter and truck driver, an experience that turned out to be, without a doubt, the best decision of my youth. As soon as I arrived, I felt an unwavering sense of belonging, as if I had finally found my place.

Since then, I have never looked back. But the quest for self is a winding path. After several years working in the automotive industry, another voice, more discreet but insistent, began to be heard. Something unexpected was calling me elsewhere…

Eight months as a reservist gave me the perspective I needed to rethink my future. It was through this reflection that I discovered the fascinating world of Data, a field where curiosity and rigor meet. A graduate of John Bryce after three months of intensive training, I am now ready to take on new challenges and seize every opportunity that comes my way. More than ever, I aspire to explore the world in all its facets, revealing its secrets through Data analysis.

About

I am 29 years old and I live in the heart of Israel, near Tel Aviv, a city in perpetual motion that reflects my state of mind. If there is a sentence that defines me, it is this one: "Capricorns are ambitious professionals who need space to surpass themselves at work. So it is better not to get in the way of these hard workers." And I can only agree with it. Work does not scare me, quite the contrary: the greater the challenges, the more my ambition grows with them.

Passionate about cars, a fan of outdoor sports, I also have a deep attachment to organization and order, elements that, for me, shape a clear mind and a balanced life.

I firmly believe that every day is a new opportunity, a blank page to write. Giving up before trying again would be madness, because as long as there is a breath of life, there is a field of possibilities to explore.
